Background

Self-driving cars are ubiquitously hailed as one of the great future technologies that can improve the safety and quality of life for millions of people. Higher levels of vehicle autonomy have the potential of reducing the number of car accidents related to risky and dangerous driver behaviours, can offer greater independence to people with disabilities and the elderly and can decrease road congestion and its effect on the environment. Nevertheless, a number of challenges have prevented the wide-spread used of fully autonomous vehicles on the streets. One such challenge is traffic road signs recognition, which I aim to explore in this project.

Dataset

In this project, a large multi-category classification benchmark dataset, i.e., the German Traffic Sign Recognition Benchmark (GTSRB) was employed.

Images

The GTSRB dataset was created via extraction and annotation of traffic road sign images from approximately 10 h of driving video footage recorded during the daytime on different road types in Germany. The data contain a total of 39’270 images of 1’309 traffic road signs (i.e., 30 images per road sign) and are representative of the visual appearance of road signs in real-world environments, i.e., their position, illumination and occlusion vary (examples provided on the right). Due to the nature of the data collection method, the size of the traffic sign images ranges between 15 × 15 and 222 × 193 pixels, which includes a 10% margin (of at least 5 pixels) around the traffic sign.
